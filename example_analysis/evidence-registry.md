## Quality Assessment

### Evidence Balance
The evidence base covers multiple perspectives with reasonable balance. The dominant narrative -- that mobile gaming expanded the overall gaming market rather than cannibalizing existing segments -- is supported by academic studies (E36, E37), industry reports (E39, E40), and market data (E02, E03). Challenging evidence includes mobile revenue stagnation (E16, E26), predatory monetization concerns (E11, E12, E13), and significant industry contraction signals such as layoffs and studio closures (E18, E19, E20). The ratio of evidence supporting market expansion versus evidence suggesting market maturation or harm is approximately 2:1, which warrants caution. Notably, evidence of mobile gaming's negative effects on game quality and developer sustainability (E12, E13, E18, E20) provides a meaningful counterweight to the growth narrative. The industry downturn evidence (E17-E20) introduces important nuance that growth in revenue does not necessarily translate to health across the ecosystem.

### Identified Gaps
- **Independent consumer survey data**: No direct consumer satisfaction or sentiment surveys were collected outside of BCG's proprietary survey
- **Regulatory perspective**: No evidence from government regulators on loot box legislation, gambling classification, or consumer protection enforcement
- **Small/indie developer economics**: Limited evidence on how mobile marketplace dynamics affect small studios specifically (only addressed tangentially in E13)
- **Long-term player health data**: No longitudinal studies on player spending behavior, addiction, or engagement sustainability
- **Platform holder financials**: No direct financial disclosures from Apple or Google regarding App Store/Play Store gaming revenue trends
- **Emerging platform data**: Limited evidence on alternative distribution channels (e.g., web stores, sideloading) beyond anecdotal mentions

### Deception Indicators
- **E27 (AppVertices)**: Marketing-oriented content with inflated projections; figures ($138B for mobile alone in 2026) conflict with more authoritative sources (Sensor Tower reports $82B). Low reliability assigned.
- **E11 (Wayline.io)**: Blog content with unattributed "industry data" claims. Low reliability assigned.
- **E34 (SQ Magazine)**: Projects $206B for mobile gaming in 2025, which significantly exceeds figures from Newzoo ($103B) and Sensor Tower ($82B), suggesting potential conflation of total gaming market with mobile-only figures. Medium reliability assigned.
- No sources were flagged for deliberate disinformation, but several lower-tier sources show evidence of promotional framing or imprecise data citation.
