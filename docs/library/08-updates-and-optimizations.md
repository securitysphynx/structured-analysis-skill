## 1. Updated Doctrine and Literature

- **Expansion**: 66 techniques (up from ~12 in the Primer), including 9 entirely new methods
- **Family Grouping**: Reorganized from 3 categories into **6 process-driven families**
- **Technique Selection Matrix**: New guidance matching technique to specific uncertainty type or cognitive bias

## 2. Technological and "Lean" Optimizations

### Lean SATs for Rapid Analysis
- Developed for fast-paced environments (watch floors, cyber incident response)
- Prioritize **high-impact, low-overhead** methods:
  - Problem Restatement
  - Chronologies
  - Key Assumptions Check
- Completable in **minutes** rather than days

### Augmented Analytics and Human-Machine Teaming (HMT)
- AI handles "administrative drag" (data intake) and "analytical drag" (pattern identification)
- Humans focus on "agentic intelligence" (strategic decision-making)
- Frameworks for integrating AI into SAT workflows

### Automated ACH Tools
- **PARC ACH 2.0**: Automated evidence weighting and visualization
- **Decision Command**: Integrated analysis platform
- **DECIDE**: Automated diagnostic trail tracking
- Replaces the manual matrices of the 2009 Primer

## 3. Empirical Studies and Critical Reviews

> **Note**: The following findings are reported in methodological reviews and meta-analyses of SAT effectiveness. Specific study designs, sample sizes, and effect magnitudes vary; readers should consult original sources for full methodological context.

### ACH Efficacy Challenges (as reported in studies 2019–2024)
- **Dhami et al.**: Mixed evidence for ACH effectiveness
- ACH may **increase judgment inconsistency and error** in probabilistic tasks
- Decomposition can **amplify "random noise"** rather than clarify signal

### The Bipolarity Problem (as reported in Dhami et al., 2024)
- Focusing on suppressing one bias (over-confidence) can trigger the opposite (under-confidence)
- Leads to "watered-down" intelligence less useful for decision-makers
- SATs lack built-in metrics for detecting over-correction

### Cognitive Costs of AI Reliance (as reported in 2024–2025 studies)
- Generative AI assists in evidence synthesis
- Over-reliance causes **"cognitive offloading"**
- Significantly reduces analyst's:
  - Baseline critical thinking scores
  - Task-specific self-confidence

## 4. Specialized Cyber Optimizations

### Stakeholder-Specific Vulnerability Categorization (SSVC)
- Modern decision-tree optimization
- Replaces traditional scoring (e.g., CVSS) with a **structured approach** to prioritizing flaws most likely to be exploited
- Aligns vulnerability management with SAT principles

### Cyber Threat Landscape (CTL) Methodology
- Explicitly pairs **Alternative Futures** with **Indicators and Warning (I&W)**
- Real-time monitoring of:
  - Zero-day persistence
  - Infrastructure-as-a-Service (IaaS) threats
- Used by CISA and ENISA for 2025 threat landscape assessments

| Period | Development |
|---|---|

| **2015** | ICD 203 revised (strengthened analytic standards) |

| **2026** | "Agile Rigor" paradigm: Lean SATs + HMT as standard practice |
