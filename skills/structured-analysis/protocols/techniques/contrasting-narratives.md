# Protocol: Contrasting Narratives

> **Phase**: Challenge & Foresight | **Effort**: Hours | **Bias Mitigated**: Narrative bias, Confirmation bias, Manipulation susceptibility
> **Output Artifact**: `working/narratives.md`
> **Library**: 00-prime §4 (Extended: Frontier), 04-agile-rigor-update

---

## Purpose

Analyze the STRUCTURE of competing stories, not just their claims. Optimized for "post-truth" environments where multiple narratives compete for acceptance — this technique maps narrative architecture, stress-tests each against evidence, and identifies vulnerabilities exploitable by disinformation.

**Use in influence operations defense**: Map the adversary's "narrative arc" to predict future disinformation "beats" and provide "pre-bunking" strategies.

---

## Execution

### 1. SETUP
- Read problem context, available evidence, and any identified adversarial narratives from prior technique outputs
- Identify the primary narrative (the prevailing explanation) and at least one competing narrative
- Gather evidence from `working/evidence-registry.md`

### 2. PRIME
State: "I will now analyze the structure of competing narratives — not just what they claim, but how they are built — to find where they break against evidence."

### 3. EXECUTE

1. **Map the primary narrative** — decompose into structural elements:
   - **Protagonist**: Who is the hero or victim?
   - **Antagonist**: Who is the villain or threat?
   - **Crisis**: What is the central problem or conflict?
   - **Resolution**: What outcome is being argued for?
   - **Evidence basis**: What facts support this structure?
2. **Map each competing narrative** — apply the same structural decomposition:
   - Use identical categories for direct comparison
   - Identify the source/origin of each competing narrative
   - Note where competing narratives share structural elements (convergence points)
3. **Stress-test each narrative against evidence** — for every key narrative element:
   - What evidence supports it?
   - What evidence contradicts it?
   - Assign a resilience rating (HIGH = strong factual support, LOW = mostly emotional/rhetorical)
4. **Identify vulnerabilities** — flag elements with:
   - HIGH emotional resonance + LOW factual support (manipulation risk)
   - These are the points where audiences are most susceptible to disinformation
5. **Develop pre-bunking strategies** — for each identified vulnerability:
   - What proactive messaging could inoculate audiences?
   - What factual counter-narrative addresses the emotional need without the false claims?
   - Prioritize strategies by impact and feasibility

### 4. ARTIFACT
Write `working/narratives.md` using the Contrasting Narratives template.

### 5. FINDINGS
Summarize: key narrative vulnerabilities (high emotion / low fact), the strongest and weakest narrative elements across all competing stories, and priority pre-bunking opportunities.

### 6. HANDOFF
Pass findings to the next technique or to final assessment compilation.

---

## Watch-Outs
- **Narrative bias**: The analyst has their own narrative preferences. Map ALL narratives with equal structural rigor, including the one you believe.
- **Fact-check tunnel vision**: This technique analyzes narrative STRUCTURE, not just individual claims. A narrative can be composed entirely of true facts and still be misleading through selective framing.
- **Emotional resonance blindness**: High emotional resonance does not mean false — but it does mean higher manipulation risk. Flag it regardless.
- **Pre-bunking overreach**: Pre-bunking strategies must be factually grounded. Fighting a false narrative with another false narrative is counterproductive.
