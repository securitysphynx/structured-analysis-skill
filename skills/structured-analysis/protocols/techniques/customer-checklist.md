# Protocol: Customer Checklist

> **Phase**: Launch | **Effort**: Minutes | **Bias Mitigated**: Type III Error (solving wrong problem)
> **Output Artifact**: `working/requirements.md`
> **Library**: 00-prime §4 (Essential 8), 01-tradecraft-primer §ICD 203, 09-core-techniques

---

## Purpose

Clarify what the consumer of this analysis actually needs. Prevent Type III Error — solving the wrong problem entirely.

---

## Execution

### 1. SETUP
- Read conversation context for the stated problem/question
- Identify who is asking and why (explicit or implied)

### 2. PRIME
State to user: "Before analyzing, I need to clarify the requirements to ensure we solve the right problem."

### 3. EXECUTE

Work through these questions systematically:

1. **Who is the consumer?** — Who will read/use this analysis?
2. **What decision will this inform?** — What action hangs on the answer?
3. **What is the timeline?** — How quickly is this needed?
4. **What format is needed?** — Brief assessment, full report, decision matrix?
5. **What is the core question?** — Restate in your own words
6. **What does the consumer already know?** — Avoid repeating known context
7. **What would change their mind?** — What evidence would shift their position?
8. **What action should they take?** — The "So What?"
9. **What are the consequences of error?** — Stakes assessment

Derive answers from conversation context. Flag any questions that cannot be answered from available context.

### 4. ARTIFACT
Write `working/requirements.md` using the Customer Checklist template.

### 5. FINDINGS
Summarize: the refined requirement, the "So What?" statement, and the stakes level.

### 6. HANDOFF
Pass requirements to Issue Redefinition for problem reframing.

---

## Watch-Outs
- **Type III Error**: The stated question is often NOT the real question. Probe for the underlying need.
- **Assumed format**: Don't assume a full report is needed — sometimes a one-paragraph assessment suffices.
- **Missing stakeholder**: If the consumer is unclear, flag this explicitly.
