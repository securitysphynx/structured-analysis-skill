# Protocol: Structured Brainstorming

> **Phase**: Exploration | **Effort**: 60–90 minutes | **Bias Mitigated**: Premature closure, groupthink
> **Output Artifact**: `working/brainstorm.md`
> **Library**: 00-prime §4 (Essential 8), 01-tradecraft-primer, 09-core-techniques

---

## Purpose

Generate a high volume of ideas through disciplined divergent-then-convergent thinking. Prevent premature closure by separating idea generation from idea evaluation.

---

## Execution

### 1. SETUP
- Read the problem framing from `working/requirements.md`
- Read the evidence registry for available source material
- Identify the focal question to be brainstormed

### 2. PRIME
State to user: "I will now run a structured brainstorm. First I will generate as many ideas as possible without filtering. Then I will group and prioritize them."

### 3. EXECUTE

**Divergent Phase:**
1. State the focal question clearly
2. Generate 15+ ideas without censorship, evaluation, or filtering
3. For each idea, note what prompted it (evidence, analogy, inversion)
4. Push through obvious ideas to reach unconventional ones
5. Include ideas from outside the immediate domain

**Convergent Phase:**
6. Group ideas by commonality into labeled clusters
7. Identify outlier ideas that resist clustering
8. Assess each cluster for relevance and novelty
9. Vote on top priorities with explicit rationale

### 4. ARTIFACT
Write `working/brainstorm.md` using the Structured Brainstorming template.

### 5. FINDINGS
Summarize: top priority clusters, most promising outlier ideas, and any unexpected themes that emerged.

### 6. HANDOFF
Pass prioritized ideas to Key Assumptions Check or ACH for diagnostic testing.

---

## Watch-Outs
- **Premature judgment**: Do NOT evaluate ideas during the divergent phase. Evaluation kills volume.
- **"Killer phrases"**: Avoid "that won't work," "we tried that," or "be realistic" during generation.
- **Stopping too early**: The best ideas often come after the obvious ones are exhausted. Push past the first pause.
- **Domain tunnel vision**: Deliberately seek analogies from unrelated fields.
